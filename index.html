Bayesian Transformer Language Modelling for Speech Recognition

Abstract: State-of-the-art neural language models (LMs) represented by Transformers are highly complex. Their use of ï¬xed, deterministic parameter estimates fail to account for model uncertainty and lead to over-ï¬tting and poor generalization when given limited training data. In order to address these issues, this paper proposes a full Bayesian learning framework for Transformer LM estimation. Efï¬cient variational inference based approaches are used to estimate the latent parameter posterior distributions associated with different parts of the Transformer model architecture including multi-head self-attention, feed forward and embedding layers. Statistically signiï¬cant word error rate (WER) reductions up to 0.5% absolute (3.18% relative) and consistent perplexity gains were obtained over the baseline Transformer LMs on state-of-the-art Switchboard corpus trained LF-MMI factored TDNN systems with i-Vector speaker adaptation. Performance improvements were also obtained on a cross domain LM adaptation task requiring porting a Transformer LM trained on the Switchboard and Fisher data to a low-resource DementiaBank elderly speech corpus.

  • Institution: CUHK

Patient-speciï¬c Fetal Heart Rate Detection based on Deep Learning

Abstract: Fetal electrocardiogram (FECG) plays an important role in fetal health during pregnancy. There are many noises and maternal electrocardiogram overlapping on FECGs collected by non-invasive monitoring, so how to detect fetal heartbeats remains a highly challenging project to date. Therefore, we attempt to propose a method for detecting fetal heart rate (FHR) with deep learning on FECG. This paper proposed a detection model based on deep learning by recognizing fetal heartbeats on input FECGs to obtain FHR. After preprocessing, a 1D CNN-LSTM architecture is utilized to detect segments that contain fetal QRS complexes as heartbeats and mark using a sliding window on given FECGs. We also proposed an ensemble learning based approach specifically fine-tuning results for each patient to achieve patient-specific diagnosis. Three FECG datasets (4-channel format, 74 women in total) are used. Proposed methods detect fetal heartbeat accurately and achieve patientspecific-level diagnosis. The accuracy of heartbeat detection and mean error of FHR are employed to evaluate the performance. 95% accuracy is achieved on Challenge 2013.
